# Pricing & Offer Learning Log

## Role

Reusable pricing memory for TimDesign offers.

This file captures real commercial learnings from client conversations, accepted offers, refused options, and pricing experiments.

It is not a CRM, not a task tracker, and not a fixed price list.

Notion remains the place for live clients, projects, missions, statuses, invoices, and follow-up.

## When To Use

Use this file when estimating, packaging, or explaining an offer, especially when:

- pricing a follow-up after an audit;
- deciding between two or three offer options;
- calibrating a client-loyalty price;
- understanding why a proposal was accepted or refused;
- turning a real client interaction into reusable commercial judgment.

## How To Use

Treat entries as precedents, not rules.

Every new quote should still be adapted to:

- actual scope;
- client relationship;
- urgency;
- technical complexity;
- writing/review effort;
- implementation effort;
- risk and responsibility level.

## Entry Criteria

Add an entry only when there is a reusable business lesson.

Do not add operational details such as task status, invoice status, reminders, delivery steps, or client follow-up. Those belong in Notion.

## Entries

### 2026-06 — Delley Boissons — Cookie audit follow-up accepted

**Context**

Long-standing client. Initial technical data protection / cookie audit had already been sold for CHF 300. The client had received the report, started reading it, then left it aside due to workload. A later human follow-up was well received; the client said the reminder was useful and expressed strong trust in Tim to apply the necessary changes.

**Offer proposed**

Two follow-up options after the audit:

- Essential option: CHF 400.
- More complete technical option: higher-priced option including cleaner third-party exposure reduction.

**Accepted**

The client accepted the CHF 400 essential option.

**Scope of accepted option**

Create and integrate a privacy policy page adapted to the site, add the footer link, and mention the relevant elements observed in the audit such as the contact form, hosting/resources, Google reCAPTCHA, Google Fonts, and technical cookies where applicable.

**Rejected / not selected**

The client did not choose the more complete technical option because he was not interested in going further than the essential compliance/transparency correction.

**Commercial lesson**

For a long-standing client, a focused post-audit essential package around CHF 400 can be accepted smoothly when:

- the audit has already established the issue;
- the next step is explained in simple language;
- the client understands this is more than a tiny free adjustment;
- the relationship includes trust;
- the option is framed as the minimum reasonable correction, not a full technical overhaul.

**Pricing lesson**

CHF 400 is a viable loyalty price for a simple essential follow-up after a prior paid audit, but it should not automatically become the default price for all cookie/privacy follow-ups.

**Limits**

Do not generalize this price too broadly. Future audit follow-ups may require custom pricing depending on:

- whether a privacy policy already exists;
- number of languages;
- analytics / pixels / marketing tools;
- need for a consent banner;
- tag blocking before consent;
- number of third-party scripts;
- Webflow implementation complexity;
- legal review expectations;
- urgency;
- client relationship.

**Reusable rule**

Use this entry as a precedent for simple post-audit follow-ups with existing clients. For new clients, more complex sites, multilingual sites, analytics-heavy setups, or consent-banner work, estimate from actual scope rather than copying this price.
